Last post, I predicted that 2021 would be loaded music-wise. Yeah...didn't happen, at least not yet.

Maybe ths system is still revving up. Maybe I'm not paying enough attention. Maybe Kendrick Lamar and the TDE conglomerate all retired and didn't tell anyone. The flood gates will open at some point, just not yet.

Still, there were a few gems and big releases worth writing about to go along with some NBA angst, as well as one of the greatest album covers of all time, so here we are! Feel free to skim and scroll through for my luke-warm takes!

Agenda: 1) NBA Thoughts 2) Olivia Rodrigo + J Cole 3) Two superlatives 4) Favorite three songs of 2021 so far.


2021 NBA Playoff Thoughts


Sure, the small-market Milwaukee Bucks eliminated the big bad Brooklyn Nets, but the war for competitive equity in the NBA has long been lost. In the era of "player empowerment" where stars cluster in big markets and force their way out of small markets, Milwaukee's struggles versus a significantly injured Brooklyn team highlights how much has to go right for small markets to hang with superteams. The Phoenix Suns had a similar breaktrough when LA Lakers star Anthony Davis was sidelined with a groin injury and LeBron was hobbled with a bum ankle and Kawhi Leonard of the LA Clippers hurt his knee. The glass cieling may only be broken by injuries.

And it's just so far gone that I've chosen to give in. I started rooting for the villainous Nets. In a league where king-making superstars LeBron James, Kevin Durant, Kawhi Leonard, and James Harden are all on their third team, you can't boycott the player empowerers and expect to be happy with the outcome. I had to turn a blind eye and just enjoy watching the Nets be the greatest offense in NBA history. They're really fun and play smart basketball! I also still enjoy watching Kawhi Leonard be a menace on both ends of the floor even though he screwed over my favorite team, the San Antonio Spurs. What I don't enjoy; watching Milwaukee star Giannis Antetokounmpo bricking ill-advised pull-up 3s and taking 15 seconds in between free throw attempts - even if he is the torchbearer for small markets everywhere.

So even in the most wide-open, small-market-friendly postseason that I can remember, I'm struggling to find a team to rally behind (although the Atlanta Hawks are making quite the Cinderella run). I realize you can only play the teams in front of you. However, am I supposed to believe that the favored Bucks and Suns would've made the NBA Finals without massive injury luck? As fun as the Hawks winning the championship would be, would anyone take it seriously? The system is so broken that parity feels like a glitch rather than a programmed outcome.

But to be honest, I'm just glad the Lakers didn't win again lol.


Olivia Rodrigo - Sour

I could continue my pessimistic energy from the previous segment, especially for an album centered around a bitter break-up, but I actually enjoy a lot of what Olivia brings to the table.

Sure, one could dismiss Sour on its face. On paper, Olivia is another product of the Disney-to-Radio pipeline who wears a lot of her influences on her sleeve. The harmonies sound like Lorde. There's Billie Eilishisms sprinkled throughout. It's Taylor Swift for the Tik Tok generation.

But what Olivia has that her predecessors don't is a freshly broken heart and the ability to turn that teenage heartbreak up to 1000. Much of Sour revolves around Olivia's emotions after getting dumped by her high-school boyfriend for a blonde girl. Whether it's the wrathful "good 4 u," to the passive-aggressive "deja vu," to the regretful second-guessing "1 step forward, 3 steps back," to the about-to-cry-any-moment "driver's license," it's as if Olivia is processing to seven stages of grief right in front of you. While I might not love every song here, I do appreciate how Sour takes its concept and paints a portrait of what Olivia's mindset.

And all that teenage melodrama feels more charming than cringey. My favorite song on the album, "favorite crime," illustrates this best. While the song's premise (i.e. hoping her ex "enjoys" the heartbreak he criminally caused her) is a bit much, its emotional climax wouldn't work if the breakup wasn't a big deal. Maybe it's the comfort of knowing that Olivia will eventually get to a point where this stuff doesn't hurt so bad. Maybe it's the nostalgia of remembering when we all were in a similar phase of teenage angst. Whatever it is, props to Olivia for a breakout album that a lot of people are resonating with.


J Cole - The Off-Season

I've come full circle on J Cole. From growing up thinking he could be the GOAT, to being lukewarm on 2014 Hills Drive, to doing a cringey economic analysis of KOD's flaws, to genuinely being excited for this project.

And after years of trying to do it all by himself, Cole is finally sticking to his strengths on "The Off-Season." There are good beats, good to great features (Lil Baby!), good raps, and no half-baked concepts. Cole is just focusing on the raps.

And while this formula works, I feel mixed on the results. On one hand, it feels like Cole read my mind and addressed every criticism that I had for KOD. Most of the songs here, especially on the second half, are pretty good. On the flip side, Cole's lack of development as an artist is pretty glaring considering where he started. There are bars, there are fire verses, but there aren't the 3-5 GREAT songs you could expect on any Cole project early in his career. Maybe my expectations are unfair.

"The Off-Season" reminds the world that Cole is really good at rapping. Mission accomplished. Yet while his technical ability has gone up this album cycle, it's small potatoes at this point of Cole's career.


Best Album Cover of the Decade


BabyTron - LUKA TRONČIĆ

I know that we still have 85% of the decade left to go, but the race is over. The best album cover of the 2020s is LUKA TRONČIĆ. The pun. The top-to-bottom color progression. The sunset and the moon at the same time. Crossing up the pawns on the chess board. The strategically placed knight piece that looks like the Dallas Mavericks mascot but avoids copyright infringement! My only complaint is that the beautifully stylized BabyTron signature fades into the background. Give the signature an outer glow or a white outline and ship it off to the Smithsonian.

Stylistically, my friend Mario and I first heard of BabyTron last night and we're still trying to figure out if he's any good. BabyTron is part of an apparently up and coming Detroit rap scene which is off-beat by design in an effort to cram as many syllables into each bar by possible. And by off-beat, I mean so off-beat he makes Blueface (who has an album called "Find the Beat") sound like a metronome.

But that's kind of the point. At times, its a repetitive onslaught, but other times, it's kinda catchy and captivating. Case in point would be the song IG Captions. BabyTron keeps the flow tighter, ups his NBA one-liner game slightly, and the result has him positively floating over an awesome, bouncy instrumental. Throw in some nicely timed beat drops, some supporting background vocals and you can really see how good this style can be when it's right.



Favorite Song of 2021 That Came Out Before 2021



Poison - Global Dan (feat. Global AzN) (2018)



    I'm not sure if Global Dan is a consistently good artist. Actually, I have a decent amount of evidence suggesting that he isn't. But having been roped into his discography by the Spotify algorithm, I'll say that he sometimes pulls out some absolute bangers with the occasional help from his friend Global AzN. For more backhanded compliments, keep reading.

    On "Poison," Mr. Dan has a pretty good hook, but AzN steals the show on the first verse. One thing to know, AzN is only capable of speaking in clichés. However, the way he strings them together on this verse is pretty clever. Even as he's generically sing-rapping about "switching lanes," "riding waves," and "making plays," it's pretty clear that he's genuinely pouring his emotional struggle, his hopes and aspirations, his everything into this single performance. It's a star-making performance dedicated to the other lost souls drifting through the winds of the YouTube algorithm.

    And yes, Global Dan's cringey Lil Uzi Vert impression on the back end doesn't quite do AzN justice, but let's stay backhandedly positive.

    "Poison" is the unapolegetic anthem of two burnouts on the eternal quest for modest internet stardom. There's a slight existentialist edge to their live-fast, die-young, maybe-get-100k-subscribers mentality as we're not sure this is all going to work out. Be that as it may, the die is cast, the lanes are switched, and in the now-immortal words of Global AzN, "you can take my heart and run away but I won't chase it."



    My Favorite Three Songs Of 2021 So Far



  1. Hooligan - Baby Keem

    2. Baby Keem's upcoming album "The Melodic Blue," is my most/only anticipated album on the horizon. Keem has a blunt, deadpan delivery that shouldn't work on paper but is ironically good, veering towards unironically good. It's hard to explain what makes his style work at times.

    Hooligan is tightly focused and has a catchy, whistling beat that stays in your head. The range of outcomes for this upcoming record feels pretty staggering. Maybe Keem's hits are flukes and this album is a boring 3-4/10. Maybe Keem is a genius and it's a 9/10 and rap AOTY. So many possibilities!

  2. Mazza - Slowthai (feat. A$AP Rocky)


    3. Mazza, short for "Mazzalean," is British slang for "madness" or general chaotic hooliganry. It's a proper descriptor for British rapper Slowthai's generally herky-jerky rap style.

    That said, "Mazza" delves into Slowthai's past struggles with drugs and depression, perhaps fueling this destructive attitude. It's a personal story told in an entertaining way. A less troubled A$AP Rocky comes in on the back end dripping with charisma in an awesome feature. A grades all around.

  3. Eœ (Disk•Initiate) - Iglooghost

    4. Less song and more cinematic mood setter, "Eœ (Disk•Initiate)" conveys a rollercoaster of emotion without saying a word. Imagine waking up on a new planet and emerging from your spaceship after a thousand years of cryo-sleep. You open the hatch and step out of the airlock to a new planet brimming with life and endless possibilities.

    It's that wondrous feeling of immersion and imagination that makes this track great. Things start harmoniously, as soaring strings accentuate the beauty that you can feel around you. Yet just as you are settling in, the winds pick up, there's a weird, swirling clicking sound, and you get a strange feeling that you aren't alone (whether that's good or bad is unclear). And then, just as you're about to reach the climax, the tension is corked, the disk is ejected, and the real world sets in. It's the ultimate tease on a hell of a teaser track.

    And sure, the rest of the album, Lei Line Eon, doesn't live up to the wonder of its first track. The album's second track, "Pure Grey Circle" pulls it off, and has the most hype first minute I've heard in a long time. Past that, the album gets somewhat bland and stale. Maybe that's to be expected as more of the world is revealed and less is left to the imagination.

    But they can't take away the first two tracks from us :''') *re-ups Spotify subscription*
