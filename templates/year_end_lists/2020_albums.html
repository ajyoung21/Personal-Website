Before we get started, here are my takes on the two blockbuster releases that I watched this year:

Tenet

Tenet very much is my kind of movie. It's plot twisty, it warps reality, it's kinda (but not really) dark. Basically it's a typical Christopher Nolan affair, and it's confusing.

As Nolan's spiritual successor to Inception. Tenet's confusing first hour and a half is surely going to turn many viewers off as Nolan slowly introduces the rules to its world. You will surely have to Google "Tenet Explained" to truly understand everything that went on during the movie.

Soul

Maybe I'm overreacting, but Soul is a top-5 Pixar movie in my book. A spiritual successor to Inside Out, Soul goes even more abstract and high-concept as its characters go on a quest to literally find the meaning of life. Yet while its overly-ambitious scope is a black hole that would crush any normal movie under the pressure of its own self-seriousness, Soul's lighthearted tone makes its complex, multi-dementional world surprisingly easy to follow. That's because Soul isn't beholden to the mechanics of its own world-building, instead using that world to visualize each character's struggle to find purpose. There's still a plot and a quest like any other movie, but its all in service to explore relatable realities that we struggle with every day.

And for a movie that explores unanswerable questions, Soul actually has some satisfying answers. It's an amazing tightrope walk of being high-concept, yet simple, exploring heavy topics with lightheartedness, providing thoughtful, nuanced truths without coming across as preachy. Pixar is even able to transition between its realistic rendering of New York City and its fantastical elements seamlessly. I can't imagine the insane degree of difficulty required to put this movie together, but Pixar makes it look easy.

Whereas Tenet's palindromic nature demands the viewer wrap its head around its self-serious reality, Soul effortlessly keeps its walking paradox of a movie easy and enjoyable to digest. Soul knows what it is. It doesn't pretend that it's targeted for kids and it doesn't claim to have life's answers. That said, it's a great deep-dive into some of life's developmental anxieties and the most enjoyable movie I've watched this year.
