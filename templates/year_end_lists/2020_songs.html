Ok, I lied, these objectively aren't the best songs of 2020. A more politically correct description would be "My Favorite Songs of 2020" or "Weird, experimental alien noises that Alex listens to." But you wouldn't haven't clicked then, would've you?

And objectively, 2020 hasn't been the best year for most people. You know the script. But in a year that's been so tough for so many people, I'm thankful that you took the time to briefly check this out. Enjoy!

Agenda: 1) Worst Songs of 2020 2) Music Video of the Year 3) Last Year's Best Songs 4) Best Songs of 2020.

  5. 4 Da Trap - 645AR

I honestly forgot that this is really how kids movies were animated. Kind of harrowing tbh.

MY FAVORITE SONGS OF 2020


  1. Dua Lipa - Love Again

    2. Dua Lipa deserves mentioning because her album had so many hits on her album that she deserved representation on the list, even if I don't have a clear-cut favorite from the album. I think at least 7 of the 11 tracks have been my favorite at some point and "Love Again" landed on top when it came to list-making time. Of all the tracks, this feels like the most 80sish throwbackish of the bunch. And the beat, weird as it might be, hits pretty hard and has grown to be my favorite on the album. The whole album slaps though so you could have thrown a dart at the tracklist and been able to justify its inclusion on this list.

  2. Dogleg - Kawasaki Backflip

    3. I actually discovered this song while waching a Tony Hawk Pro Skater YouTube video. And while the song did not inspire me to pull off a 2080 million point combo like the author of the video, it did introduce me to a high-energy, garage-rock jam. That's where music discovery is in 2020.

  3. Lupe Fiasco - Dinosaurs

    4. A purely lyrical exercise for the sake of lyrical exercises, Dinosaurs is another expression of how Lupe Fiasco has continued to level up his skills over the years. One ever-so-slight criticism that I've had about Lupe's later work is just how serious and deliberate and impenetrable his lyrical delivery has been, as if it the lyrics are buckling under the weight of all the thought that went into them. The lightheartedness of Dinosaurs' subject matter frees up Lupe to take up a free-wheeling, conversational flow over a beat so abstract that most rappers wouldn't know where to start with. It's a the tightrope of making the track sound easy, but also making it lyrically dense so that the listener picks up a new rhyme or nugget with each listen. The degree of difficulty is on full display and it's a marvel to listen to.

  4. Charli XCX - Forever

    5. COVID-19's impact on popular culture and music was inescapeable and overwhelmingly not good. Whether it was top-tier artists (@Kendrick Lamar) delaying albums due to an inability to tour to every rapper trying their darndest to force in words that rhymed with "coronavirus" into their verses, the unsolicited urge to react to COVID-19 was one of 2020 music's more annoying subplots.

    Yet of all the artists who felt compelled to relate their material to COVID-19, Charli XCX's anthem 'Forever' comes across as the most tasteful attempt by far. 'Forever' is a love song for those who are separated by the quarantine. It conveys the feeling of every romantic and familial relationship that just became long-distance. While we can't hug on Zoom, we still have so much life to live together, even if we're not in the same room.

  5. Dorian Electra - F The World (feat. The Garden, Quay Dash & d0llywood1)

    6. Perhaps the most "extra" song that I heard all year. From the hyperdrive production to the edgy dark theme, this song is out of control. Once you get to Quay Dash's verse, there's a surreal, novel feeling that you're in uncharted waters.

    Thematically, this track is a not so subtle satirization of "toxic masculinity." The album's concept portrays a man closed off from society, feeling an intense anger at what the world has become, yet still feeling a vulnerable craving to connect with others. I'll discuss a little more in the "Albums of the Year" but just know from the lyrics it's more of a "love"/hate relationship rather than just hate.

    And yet, despite its in-your-face production and chorus, it's the little hints and "chef's kiss" production flourishes that make the track. This is hard to describe, but there's specifically a little horn-like "bur br brr, br br br" that is pleasing to the ear. It's just indicative of how much effort went into this track and how much thought Dorian Electra puts into their music.

  6. Kali Uchis - quiero sentirme bien

    7. This song has my favorite chorus that I heard this year. The chorus is just so sweet and warm and I liken it to a flower blooming before your eyes, even though that same warmth and coziness just makes you want to curl up under a blanket in front of a fireplace. It's summer and fall at the same time, and one of my favorite songs to listen to and just feel good.

  7. 100 Gecs - ringtone (Remix) (feat. Charli XCX, Kero Kero Bonito, and Rico Nasty)

    8. The more time that goes by, the more convinced I am that 100 Gecs' debut album "1000 Gecs," is a classic. That release was a Big Bang (or Big Gec, if you will) that made the "Hyperpop" genre one of the hottest underground movements in music right now. Its zany, distorted pop excess has paved the way for smaller artists such as Dorian Electra while also influencing rising pop stars with actual name recognition like Charli XCX.

    I say all that because the magnitude of Gecs' influence didn't occur to me until this song dropped and I looked at the feature list. Sure, Charli XCX, KKB and Rico Nasty aren't all pop superstars (although Charli's getting close), but this is the 100 Gecs' equivalent of that 'Forever' song about LeBron James which had Drake, Kanye West, Lil Wayne and Eminem all on the same track purely for the hype.

    Because 'ringtone' remix is no longer a quaint, wholesome love song; it's now a posse track. Ironically, Sarah Bonito from Kero Kero Bonito, who's whole brand is built around wholesomeness, is here to drop some bars. It's a complete reimagining of what the original song, and what a remix in general, can be. While I go to sleep every night in fear that the Hyperpop bubble is going to pop any day now, it's this song specifically that keeps me warm knowing that the genre has legs.

  8. Juice WRLD - Man of the Year

    9. *Please note: There are two versions of this song. I much prefer the original which is not the one streaming on Spotify. Please click the hyperlink above or visit YouTube if you want to hear this original version.*

    Juice WRLD was my favorite artist in 2019, and the news of his untimely passing a year ago is the hardest I've taken a celebrity death, for what it's worth. While I was initially mildly disappointed in listening through the first 20 tracks of Juice WRLD's posthumous album this last song made the whole listening experience more that worth it.

    And while I can't give a musical justification for this song being this high on the list - it's a happy-go-lucky tune that may come across as cheesy in other contexts - something about this song hit me emotionally hard. "Man of the Year" expresses the joy of his music foiled by a few lyrics hinting at how emotionally troubled Jarad (Juice WRLD) Higgins appeared to be. It's a microcosm of what makes his tragedy hard to stomach, even as his posthumous songs were predicting it left and right.

    Although bittersweet, "Man of the Year" is one last happy goodbye from a truly talented artist. Rest in peace, Jarad.

  9. (Tie) Gupi and Fraxiom - Thos Moser and Mos Thoser

    10. Born from Gec, molded by memes, fired through the brimstone of 2020, Gupi and Fraxiom (known together as the group "food house") rose through the ashes with the sole purpose of stretching the "Hyperpop" genre to it's only logical, insane conclusion. Or actually they're probably just buddies that had a free Saturday and messed around making music and seeing what stuck. It's probably the latter. Still, they somehow hit gold.

    At its heart, "Thos Moser" is a wholesome pop song centered around organizing a spontaneous meeting with that special someone in your life. If you try to dig any deeper, you'll be met with distortion, random lyrics about Elon Musk, and overall immature silliness.

    But man does the music sound good. Fraxiom's vocal inflections combined with Gupi's dynamic production just make for fun, zany, catchy songs. Also, the end of "Mos Thoser" was the biggest musical plot twist I've heard in a while.

    One thing to not my past lists of favorite songs/albums have been historically heavy on hip-hop/rap. In 2020, my listening habits are way different. Part of it is all the top established rap artists (*cough cough Kendrick, Kanye, J Cole, all of TDE, Drake to a certain extent, Travis Scott is now a McDonald's/PS5/Nike salesman) basically punted to 2021 due to COVID. But part of it is because the Gecsverse has totally sucked me in. Shout out to Gupi and Fraxiom for helping save 2020 for me.

    *Fun Fact: Gupi is Tony Hawk's son lol.

  10. The Strokes - Ode to the Mets

    11. I bounced between "At The Door" and this song as my favorite from the album.

  11. Hand Crushed By A Mallet - 100 Gecs (Fallout Boy Remix)

    12. So I'm listening to the new 100 Gecs remix album for the first time while walking through the grocery store with my mask on. It's not my usual grocery store, so tracking down all of the produce in an unfamiliar store is a real pain. I'm taking a 3rd loop around because I'm looking for shallots and my pride won't let myself ask for help and

    wait... is that.. Fallout Boy?? Am I listening to the right song? "Oh, hello sir, sorry to bother you, but I have a soup recipe that I'm working on an do you know where I could possibly find some Fallout Boy?" (DAMNIT)

    As I walked in shame to see that the shallots were right behind the cherry tomatoes and that I had passed them four times, I could not help but smile at the smirk. Not because sheer ridiculousness of the feature choice, but because it fit

    Much like the 'ringtone' remix discussed earlier, 100 Gecs takes a very liberal interpretation on what a remix can be. They turn what was a hyperdrive electronic music banger into the slow-developing emo-punk Fallout Boy anthem it apparently was always destined to be. Once Patrick Stump takes over the track around 1:27 (my favorite musical moment of 2020), nobody is safe, and the world is better off for it.

    And even then, I still have trouble deciding between this 'hand crushed' remix and the also excellent N0thanky0u Remix that seems to operate on the assumption that the Fallout Boy remix didn't go nearly hard enough. I guess I finally get the appeal of metal music now. I kind of wish that I could take the intro from the N0thanky0u remix, take the middle to end of the Fallout Boy remix, and somehow incorporate the EDM beat from the original all into one giant Franken-song.

    Sorry I wasn't sure if I had enough 100 Gecs-type representation on my list.


    Conclusion:


    2020 had a lot of terrible surprises. While music didn't make up any of that gap, I think it was a year where a lot of great music came from unexpected places. Who knew that Dua Lipa would become pop music's quarantine overlord overnight? Who knew that that Chinese app called TikTok would have so much influence on which songs topped the Billboard charts? Who knew that three of the biggest rap albums of 2020 (Mac Miller, Pop Smoke, and Juice WRLD), would be posthumous releases and that it would have some of each artist's best songs?

    In an unconventional year, the stage was cleared for some unconventional artists to shine. They filled in admirably in what was supposed to be a "down" year. And with a light at the end of the tunnel and several gigabytes of highly anticipated, unreleased music on deck, 2021 is going to be loaded.
