Ok, I lied, these objectively aren't the best songs of 2020. A more politically correct description would be "My Favorite Songs of 2020" or "Weird, experimental alien noises that Alex listens to." But you wouldn't haven't clicked then, would've you?

And objectively, 2020 hasn't been the best year for most people. You know the script. But in a year that's been so tough for so many people, I'm thankful that you took the time to briefly check this out. Enjoy!

Agenda: 1) Worst Songs of 2020 2) Music Video of the Year 3) Last Year's Best Songs 4) Best Songs of 2020.

The Worst Songs of 2020 In My Honest Opinion:

  1. Yummy - Justin Bieber

    2. Idk, I just find the part where Justin says "yeah babe" repeatedly to be pretty dang annoying. Subject matter isn't fun for anyone besides Justin. It wins the crown for annoying radio hit that was absolutely everywhere. But you know what, the Florida Georgia Line remix is actually kinda hard even though the rap verses are hilariously absolute trash.

  2. Toosie Slide - Drake

    3. I get what you're trying to do here Drake. Drake + Tik Tok dance = Certified #1 hit. I just wish that Drake's dance and song by extension weren't so low-effort. That's all.

  3. Intentions - Justin Bieber (feat. Quavo)

    4. Upon re-listening, this song isn't as painfully bland as I remember. Beat is kinda nice and bubbly and it's not as long as I remember it. But man, some really cringey bars by Justin. "Shout out to your mom and dad for making you." "You just got that ring. Just like Toronto." The Quavo feature feels tacky. Maybe it's not as terrible as I'm making it seem, sorry Justin.

  4. Make Em Say - NLE Choppa (feat. Mulatto)

    5. Hands down the worst actual song I heard this year. The chorus is terrible and repetitive and lasts forever. Ya.

  5. 4 Da Trap - 645AR

    6. I listened to 10 seconds of this and hated it. My brother listened to 5 seconds and hated it. My dog listened to 2 seconds and started barking, which is too bad because my dog appears to be the intended audience for 645AR's obscenely high-pitched, indecipherable vocals. Look I get it, rapping is a saturated market and he's just trying to have a gimmick to get on the map, but this isn't a real song. The vocals aren't even on beat. It's utterly unlistenable and this man must be stopped.

Favorite Music Video of 2020: The Strokes - At The Door

It's hard to rate music videos because A) I only watch music videos if I really like the song and B) I have a dark, niche taste so I tend to skew for that aesthetic. "At The Door" nails both of these with an added nostalgia factor by animating the video in an 80s animation style inspired by movies like "Watership Down." I honestly forgot that this is really how kids movies were animated. Kind of harrowing tbh.

Runners Up: 1) Dua Lipa - Physical (Wild choreography and production budget), 2) The Weeknd - Faith (Crazy one-take performance)

MY FAVORITE SONGS OF 2020


  1. Dua Lipa - Love Again

    2. This one was hard because Dua Lipa had so many hits on her album that it was hard to choose. I think at least 7 of the 11 tracks have been my favorite at some point and "Love Again" landed on top when we hit list-making time. Of all the tracks, this feels like the most 80sish throwbackish of the bunch. And the beat, weird as it might be, has grown to be my favorite on the album. The whole album slaps though so you could have thrown a dart at the tracklist and been able to justify its inclusion on this list.

  2. Hand Crushed By A Mallet - 100 Gecs (Fallout Boy Remix)

    3. Pardon the wack music video.
